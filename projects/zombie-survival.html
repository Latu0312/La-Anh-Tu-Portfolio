OVERVIEW

Zombie Survival: Last Night is a fast-paced top-down survival shooter where players fight through relentless waves of zombies in a post-apocalyptic city. Combining responsive twin-stick controls, dynamic enemy AI, progressive difficulty, and an upgrade-driven progression system, the game delivers an intense combat experience focused on movement, positioning, and long-term survival. Players collect resources, unlock stronger weapons, upgrade character abilities, and adapt their strategy against increasingly dangerous enemy types, culminating in challenging boss encounters.

MY ROLE & RESPONSIBILITIES

This was a solo project — I was responsible for the entire game development pipeline from concept to final build, including:

  • Game Design: Designed the wave-based difficulty curve, enemy variety, and weapon balancing to keep the gameplay loop engaging over long sessions.
  • Programming: Implemented player movement and shooting, enemy AI and pathfinding, the wave spawner system, and the in-game HUD (health, ammo, wave counter).
  • Level Design: Laid out the survival arena, obstacle placement, and enemy spawn points to control pacing and difficulty.
  • Optimization: Used object pooling for bullets and enemies, and profiled the game to keep performance stable at 60fps even with many zombies on screen.

CASE STUDY

The Challenge

Keep players engaged across many waves without the gameplay feeling repetitive, while running real-time enemy AI for dozens of zombies without frame drops.

The Approach

Designed a wave-scaling system that gradually introduces new enemy types and increases spawn rate over time, paired with A* pathfinding and object pooling to keep AI performant at scale.

The Result

A wave-based survival loop that stays challenging and engaging across 10+ waves, running consistently at 60fps even with a large number of enemies active at once.
