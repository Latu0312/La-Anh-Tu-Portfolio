OVERVIEW

Pass The Beer is a casual arcade game that challenges players to manage a crowded bar by accurately serving drinks through physics-based throwing mechanics. Combining quick reflexes, memory, precision, and time management, the game creates fast-paced gameplay where players must identify customer orders, select the correct beverage, and deliver it accurately before customers lose patience.


Designed for short mobile play sessions, the game emphasizes satisfying one-touch controls while gradually increasing cognitive and mechanical difficulty as more customers, drink types, and simultaneous orders appear.

MY ROLE & RESPONSIBILITIES

This was a solo project — I handled every part of the game's development, including:

  • Game Design: Designed the one-touch core loop, timing windows, and difficulty progression to keep the game addictive in short sessions.
  • Programming: Built the physics-based beer-passing mechanics with realistic momentum and collision response, plus the scoring and combo system.
  • UI/UX: Designed mobile-friendly touch controls and a clean, readable HUD suited for quick casual play.
  • Optimization: Tuned the physics simulation to run smoothly on low-end mobile devices without sacrificing feel.

CASE STUDY

The Challenge

Make a physics-based interaction feel satisfying and responsive, while keeping performance smooth on low-end mobile hardware.

The Approach

Simplified the physics simulation to only what the gameplay needed, and carefully tuned timing windows and feedback through repeated playtesting.

The Result

A simple, addictive core loop that feels responsive and satisfying even on budget devices, with a difficulty curve that keeps players coming back.
